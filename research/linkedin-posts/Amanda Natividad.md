## Summary
LinkedIn determines whether a post appears in users' feeds by first evaluating its relevance to the audience and then ranking it based on engagement and quality signals. Posts that are relevant, receive strong engagement, and keep users interested are more likely to be shown to a wider audience.

Key Takeaways:

LinkedIn first decides if your post is relevant to a user's feed.
Relevance depends on factors like your network, profile, content topic, and audience interests.
Only relevant posts move to the ranking stage.
